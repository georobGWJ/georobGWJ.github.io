Evaluation of a Caltrans Bay Bridge Website

For this evaluation, I looked at the Caltrans site THE SAN FRANCISCO/OAKLAND BAY BRIDGE

What makes this site good or bad?

The content on this site provides a useful overview of the history of the Bay Bridge and of some of the key facts about it. However, the formatting on this webpage is very simple and that makes it difficult to mentally parse in some sections. The Facts at a Glance section is the most egregious; it is difficult to easily see where information about one structure ends and another begins. I tried to make this a little clearer on my main page by using tables and lists.

Another issue with the website is that the information is not meaningfully semantically labeled with tags. All of the historical section labels are paragraphs with bold text tags; for example:

< p >< b >CONSTRUCTING THE IMPOSSIBLE< /b >< /p >

It would be more semantically meaningful to tag these as headers or possibly even as sections.

Lastly, there is no navigational data on the site. It doesn't point back to its parent site or to any references.
